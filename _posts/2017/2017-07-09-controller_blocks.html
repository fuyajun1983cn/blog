## Device Manager * the functional block in the baseband that controls the general behavior of the Bluetooth device. * responsible for all operations not directly related to data transport such as: * Inquiry * Connect * make local device discoverable or connetable.
## Device Manager * need access to the transport medium to carry out its functions. * controls local device behavior by a number of the HCI commands such as: * manage local name * store link keys. * other functionality.
## Link Manager * responsible for managing logical links(or related logical transports): * creation * modification * release * update of parameters related to physical links between devices. * using LMP in BR/EDR and LL in LE to achieve the above functions.
## Link Manager * LM or LL protocol allows: * creation of new logical links and logical transports when required. * the general control of link and transport attributes such as: * enabling of encryption on the logical transport * adapting of TX power in BR/EDR on the physical link. *adjusting of QoS Settings in BR/EDR for a logical link.
## Baseband Resource Manager * responsible for all access to the radio medium. * has two main functions: * a scheduler that grants time on the physical channels. * negotiate access contracts with other entities. * An access contract is effectively a commitment to deliver a certain QoS reuirement. * realignment time between slots on the original physical channel and slots on the new physical channel.
## Link Controller * responsible for the encoding and decoding of Bluetooth packets from data payload. * and parameters related to the physical channel, logical transport and logical link. * carry out link control protocol signaling in BR/EDR and link layer protocol in LE.
## PHY * responsible for transmitting and receiving packets of information on the physical channel.
